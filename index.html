Ganga Aarti,Varanasi

The Ganga Aarti on banks of holy river Ganga

Dal Lake,Srinagar

Dal is a lake in Srinagar,the summer capital of Jammu and Kashmir.

Golden Temple

The Golden Temple,or the Darbar Sahib,is a gurdwara located in the city of Amritsar, Punjab.

Durga Puja,West Bengal

Durga Puja, also known as Durgotsava or Sharodotsav, is an annual festival originating in the Indian subcontinent which reveres and pays homage to the Hindu goddess Durga, and is also celebrated because of Durga's victory over Mahishasura.

Placeholder Bihar

Bihar is a place full of history and spiritual significance. Located in the eastern part of India, this state is known as the land of monasteries. There are many things that make this place worth visiting.

Placeholder Karnataka

Karnataka, the sixth largest state in India, has been ranked as the third most popular state in the country for tourism in 2014.The ancient sculptured temples,hill ranges, forests and beaches are some tourism centers. .

Uttarakhand

The mystical land of mountains and mythologies, exquisite landscapes and exhilarating adventure, and wellness and yoga, Uttarakhand has something to offer every traveller. Popularly known as Devbhoomi.

Placeholder Arunachal Pradesh

Popularly known as ‘Land of Dawn-lit-Mountains’, Arunachal Pradesh is India’s remotest state and the first Indian soil to greet the rising sun. Located on the northeastern tip of India with its borders touching China, Bhutan and Burma this beautiful land is endowed with dazzling array of flora and fauna that is sure to allure any tourist.

Placeholder Jammu & Kashmir

Jammu & Kashmir, a piece of heaven on earth, is the 19th largest state in India and it is located mostly all in the Himalayan mountains. It is also the only state in India with a Muslim-majority population. Each of these regions has its own culture, and this is evident in their daily lives and also their lifestyle.

Placeholder Lakshadweep

The unique feature of the Islands is its coral reef, making it a pristine leisure spot to come back to. 4200 sq. kms. of lagoon, rich in marine wealth, is spread over 36 islands in an area of 32 sq. kms. The underwater view at Lakshadweep is kaleidoscopic and breathtaking. The lagoon offers excellent potential for water sports like swimming.
